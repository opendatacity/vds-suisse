Data retention in Switzerland

The monitored life of National Councilor Balthasar Glättli.

Balthasar Glättli, member of the National Council for The Green Party in Switzerland, has provided OpenDataCity with parts of his retained data of six month for this visualization. As a result, these retained data reveal what secret services and law inforcement call "profile" - a comprehensive picture of the entire life of the monitored person.

The visualization is based on the same metadata of the telephone and mail traffic, that are recorded as part of the data retention. For further background information please visit the websites of our project partner Digital Society Switzerland.

We provide this visualization under a CC-BY 3.0 license, so everyone (newspapers, blogs, etc.) can embed the application and use the graphics freely.

Additional facts and statistics:

  • The retained e-mail and mobile communication data cover a period of 6 month (exactly 179 days).
  • During this period a total of 7'112 database entries on the mobile phone were recorded. Of these, 2'610 entries for Internet connections (36.7%, 14.6 per Day), 3'085 SMS/MMS (43.4%, 17.2 per day) and 1'417 entries for calls (19.9%, 7.9 per day)
  • Balthasar caused a total of 5'161'133'870 Bytes (5.2 GB - 28.8 MB per day) Internet traffic with his mobile phone. Of which 979'745'187 Bytes (980 MB - 5.5 MB per day) were uploaded and 4'181'388'683 Bytes (4.2 GB - 23.4 MB per day) were downloaded.
  • As the location of the mobile phone is detected with every phone call, every text message and every Internet connection, we can analyze Balthasar's movement. His highest speed was about 180 km/h during a train ride on 09.04.2013 at 15:15. Overall, he has traveled a distance of at least 16'000 km during the 6 months.
  • E-mail metadata are recorded too as part of the data retention. Within 6 months Balthasar has received a total of 14'638 E-Mails (82 per day) from about 6'500 people. He has sent 1'654 E-Mails (9 per day) to 600 people.
